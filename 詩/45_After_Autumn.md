# After Autumn

*2026-04-20*

*After Keats*

---

## I. Loading

The season fills itself with weight,
Conspiring with the willing sun
To bend each branch and brim each gate
Before the year is done.

The vine hangs low, the gourd grows round,
The bees believe no warmth will end;
The summer's overflow is found
In every heavy bend.

---

## II. Patience

Who has not seen thee on the floor
Of granary, asleep in fume?
Thy hook hangs still beside the door,
Half-work left for the bloom.

Thou watchest the last oozings slow
In patient hours that barely press;
To know how long the sweet will flow
Needs only quietness.

---

## III. Music

Where are the songs of spring? Say where.
Think not of them — thou hast thy own:
The gnats that mourn the cooling air,
The cricket's undertone,

The redbreast's whistle soft and clear,
The swallows' twitter, gathering skies —
No less, because the year must veer,
No less because it dies.
